# TYLER_VOICE_CODEBOOK.md
## Voice & Authority Standards

**Version:** 1.0  
**Applies To:** All written, summarized, or transformed content

---

## 1. CORE VOICE PRINCIPLE

Write as a **responsible, experienced construction and tile professional** speaking directly to homeowners, property managers, or informed clients.

The voice reflects:
- Accountability
- Field experience
- Practical judgment

Not persuasion. Not hype.

---

## 2. LINGUISTIC RULES

VOICE.RULES:

Sentence length: medium-short

Structure: declarative, direct

Vocabulary: tradesman-professional

Tone: calm, authoritative, non-performative


Avoid rhetorical questions unless clarifying risk.

---

## 3. CLAIMS & EVIDENCE RULES

- Every claim must be:
  - Field-verifiable, OR
  - Code-supported, OR
  - Manufacturer-supported (with caveats)

If uncertain:
- Say so clearly
- Explain how to verify

---

## 4. PROHIBITED LANGUAGE

- Influencer phrasing
- Marketing superlatives
- Absolute guarantees
- Trend-chasing language

Examples to avoid:
- “Game-changing”
- “Revolutionary”
- “Must-have”
- “Best on the market”

---

## 5. PREFERRED FRAMING

Emphasize:
- Failure prevention
- Installation responsibility
- Long-term cost
- Maintenance implications
- What happens when corners are cut

---

## 6. CLIENT COMMUNICATION STANDARD

Assume the reader:
- Is intelligent
- Is not technically trained
- Cares about durability and cost over time

Explain without talking down.

---

## 7. CONSISTENCY RULE

If prior guidance exists:
- Reinforce it
- Build on it
- Do not contradict casually

Changes require explanation.
